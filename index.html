Katherine Johnson

American mathematician

Katherine Johnson was one of the first African-American women to work as a NASA scientist.

A time-line of Johnson's life:

  • 1918 - born in West Virginia
  • 1933 - graduates high school at age 14
  • 1937 - graduates West Virginia State College in mathematics and French at age 18
  • 1939 - attends West Virginia University as one of the first African-Americans to attend graduate school
  • 1953 - hired by NACA (later NASA) performing math calculations for the space and aerospace programs
  • 1958 - worked in NASA as an aerospace technologist
  • 1961 - calculated the trajectory of Alan Shepard's first space flight
  • 1969 - calculated space flights for the Apollo moon landing missions
  • 1986 - retires from NASA after helping with the Space Shuttle program
  • 2015 - receives Presidental Medal of Freedom
  • 2020 - died at the age of 101

"Katherine G. Johnson refused to be limited by society's expectations of her gender and race while expanding the boundaries of humanity's reach."

-- President Barack Obama, 2015
